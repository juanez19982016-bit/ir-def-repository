## 💡 Pricing Strategies That Work

### 1. The "Three Options" Strategy
Always present 3 pricing tiers. Most clients pick the middle one:

- **Basic** (low, anchoring) → Ensures you don't lose the deal
- **Standard** (your target) → What you actually want to sell
- **Premium** (high, anchoring) → Makes Standard look reasonable

### 2. Value-Based Pricing
Don't charge by hours. Charge by the **value** you create:

> "If this e-commerce site will generate $50K/month for the client,
> charging $10K is a bargain for them — even if it takes you 2 weeks."

## 🚫 Common Pricing Mistakes

1. **Charging too little** — Underpricing attracts bad clients who don't respect your work
2. **Not charging for revisions** — Always define revision limits in your contract
3. **Working without a deposit** — NEVER start without at least 50% upfront
4. **Scope creep without re-quoting** — "Oh, can you also add X?" = new quote
5. **Not raising rates** — Increase rates by 10-20% every year

## 🔢 Quick Client Qualification Questions

Ask these BEFORE quoting:
1. What's your budget range? (Filters out time-wasters)
2. What's the deadline? (Rush = higher rate)
3. Do you have designs ready? (No design = add design cost)
4. What similar sites do you like? (Understand expectations)
5. Is this a new project or redesign? (Redesigns are harder)
