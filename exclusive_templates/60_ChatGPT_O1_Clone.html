RSA encryption relies on the mathematical properties of prime numbers and modular arithmetic. Here is a step-by-step example using very small numbers to keep the math easy to follow.

1. Key Generation

First, we select two distinct prime numbers, p and q. Let's choose:
p = 3
q = 11

We multiply them to get our modulus n:
n = 3 * 11 = 33

Next, we calculate the totient (the number of integers smaller than n that share no common factors with n):
φ(n) = (p-1) * (q-1) = 2 * 10 = 20
